# ADR 004 — ReWear Personas

**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Status:** Locked v1
**Decided in:** Session 1

This doc names the people we're building for. Used as a decision filter ("would Maya understand this? would Maya screenshot it?"). Lives forever.

---

## Primary persona — Maya

**Maya · 26 · East London · Senior content designer at a fintech**

Earns ~£42k. Rents a one-bed in Hackney. Vegetarian. Reads Vittles, watches *Sort Your Life Out*, has a Vinted account she doesn't really use. Spends her Sundays on Pinterest, her Sunday nights on Instagram, and her commute on Substack.

She has about 180 items in her wardrobe. She wears around 40 of them. The rest are: that dress she bought for a wedding in 2022, three jumpers from her ex's mum, four tops she keeps because *"they were £80"*, a Cos coat she's actually obsessed with, two thrifted blazers, and the bottom drawer of *"I'll figure these out one day."*

She's not preachy about sustainability. She just feels quietly bad about Shein, won't buy fast fashion, and checks the Good On You rating before any new purchase — which she makes maybe four times a year. She'd love to thrift more but doesn't have the patience.

### Her real frustration

It's not the planet. Not in the morning, anyway. It's **her wardrobe being full and useless.** She rotates the same six outfits. She knows there's more in there. She just can't *see* it.

### What she's tried, and why each thing failed

| Tool | Why it lost her |
|---|---|
| Pinterest | Pretty but unactionable. Outfits with clothes she doesn't own. |
| Whering | Opened it once, felt try-hard, never came back. |
| A Notion wardrobe doc | Quit after 12 items. |
| Capsule wardrobe newsletters | Too rigid. Too austere. |
| Vinted | Hated photographing and listing. |

### Why ReWear lands

- It uses the clothes she **actually owns**, not aspirational ones.
- It tells her something **specific** each morning (*"the green knit, the dark jeans, the white trainers"*).
- It quietly reduces her shopping without making her feel like a member of a movement.
- When she does want to buy, the eco-brands directory is curated, not preachy.
- The all-in-one promise replaces five tabs (Pinterest, Vinted, YouTube, Google Maps, eco-brand sites).

### What loses her instantly

- Anything that lectures her about climate
- Anything that asks her to "join the movement"
- Too many emoji
- Anything that sounds American
- Anything that calls her "babe" or "bestie"

### The line she'd screenshot to a group chat

> *"You don't have nothing to wear. You have 217 things you've forgotten."*

### Where she hangs out (channels we should be on)

- **Instagram** — primary discovery, especially Reels of "wardrobe of the day" creators
- **TikTok** — selectively, when the algorithm shows her sustainable fashion / outfit-formula creators (Sophie Silva, Mina Le, Brigette Pheloung)
- **Substack** — reads design and culture writers
- **Pinterest** — saves outfits, never finds time to recreate them
- **Charity shops on Sundays** — occasional, in person

## Secondary persona — Sarah (briefly)

**Sarah · 38 · two kids (7 and 4) · suburban**

Different problem: outgrown clothes, hand-me-down logistics, school-uniform churn. Same emotional core: overwhelmed by stuff, doesn't want to throw it out, wants a smarter system.

She's not the focus for v1. Worth a full persona doc later if/when we lean into this segment.

---

## How to use these personas

1. **In design reviews:** *"Would Maya screenshot this? Would Maya delete the app over this?"*
2. **In copy reviews:** *"Does this sound like someone Maya would forward to a group chat — or someone she'd block?"*
3. **In feature decisions:** *"Does this solve Maya's morning, or our internal ambition?"*
4. **In voice work:** *"Would Maya cringe at this sentence?"*
