# ADR 001 — Positioning angle

**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Status:** Locked
**Decided by:** Anna-Liv (founder), with JG (sponsor) — live in Session 1

## Context

Three positioning angles were evaluated:

1. **The Anti-Closet-Crisis** — lead with the universal "I have nothing to wear" problem. AI fixes it. Eco is bonus.
2. **The Style Loop** — only app that follows a garment through its whole life (wear → restyle → upcycle → resell → donate). Strong differentiation vs. Whering.
3. **The Creative Wardrobe** — Pinterest energy. Wardrobe as a creative project. Identity-led.

## Decision

**Synthesis: "The Everything Wardrobe."**

Combine the hook from Angle 1 with the promise from Angle 2:

- **Hook (the problem):** *"You don't have nothing to wear. You have 217 things you've forgotten."*
- **Promise (the everything-in-one-place):** Outfits + upcycling + resell + donation + eco-brands, all in one app — instead of juggling Pinterest, Vinted, YouTube, and separate eco-sites.
- **Mission (the why):** *Wear More. Waste Less.* (still threaded through, never preached)

Anna-Liv's phrasing: *"I like the problem-solving of 'I don't have anything to wear' in front of your full closet, but I also like the idea where it's focused on 'we have everything', so instead of having all these different apps, we have everything here in one place."*

## Locked hero direction (from 3 options)

**Anna-Liv chose Option A:**

> *"Open your closet, not another app."*
> Everything for your wardrobe — outfits, upcycling, reselling, donating. One place.

## Why we did *not* pick Angle 3 (Creative Wardrobe)

Anna-Liv didn't pick the Pinterest-led creative-identity angle even though I recommended it. Her instinct was utility + breadth, not creative-lifestyle. Pinterest stays as an *experiential* reference (browsable, image-led, masonry-friendly UX) but is no longer the *brand identity* anchor.

## Consequences for downstream work

- **Voice (Session 1, next):** confident, declarative, slightly clever, never preachy. Anna-Liv's pick of Option A signals she likes a confident, witty, modern register.
- **Visual identity (Session 2):** clean, minimal, earthy palette stays. Patchwork-recycle logo concept stays — it now reads as "the everything wardrobe" (different fabrics woven into one mark).
- **Marketing site (Session 5):** hero leads with the chosen line, supporting copy explains the loop. Eco mission below the fold or in a dedicated "why" section, not preached up top.
- **App home screen (Session 7):** problem-solving framing on first launch ("Here's an outfit from your closet today"). Other modules accessible from one place.
- **Competitive frame:** ReWear is positioned against *fragmentation* (Pinterest + Vinted + YouTube + Google Maps + brand sites), not against Whering directly. Whering is just a wardrobe app — ReWear is everything-for-your-closet.
