# ADR 003 — ReWear Manifesto v1

**Date:** 2026-04-29
**Status:** Draft (awaiting Anna-Liv approval)
**Length:** ~115 words
**Voice:** per ADR 002 (cool older sister + small streak of mischief)

This is the "why we exist" piece. It lives on the website's About page, in the app's first onboarding screen, on the inside of the press kit, and as the answer to *"so what is ReWear?"*

---

## The manifesto

> Open your closet. There's nothing to wear.
>
> It's a lie your closet's been telling you for years. The truth is, you've got hundreds of things in there — most of them underused, half-forgotten, occasionally regretted. You don't need more clothes. You need a better way to use the ones you already own.
>
> That's all ReWear is, really. Outfits from what you have. Restyle the bits you're bored of. Resell what no longer fits. Donate what you can't justify keeping. And when you do want something new — we'll point you at brands worth buying from.
>
> One closet. One app. The whole thing.
>
> Wear More. Waste Less. It's that simple — and that satisfying.

---

## Notes for downstream use

- **Hero of marketing site:** opens with the first paragraph (or just the first two sentences as a pull quote)
- **About page:** runs the whole manifesto
- **App onboarding screen 1:** uses lines 1–2 ("Open your closet. There's nothing to wear. It's a lie your closet's been telling you for years.")
- **Press / one-pager:** runs the manifesto verbatim
- **Social bio (Instagram / TikTok):** *"Open your closet, not another app. Wear more, waste less."*

## What it deliberately does NOT do

- Doesn't shout about climate, microplastics, or fast fashion stats
- Doesn't preach
- Doesn't say "we believe…" or "our mission is to…"
- Doesn't promise a revolution
- Doesn't ask the reader to feel guilty
